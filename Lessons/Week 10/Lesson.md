# Week 10 Lesson Plan  
## Inquiry Workshop: Interpreting Results and Preparing Presentations  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- describe patterns in their data  
- interpret results carefully and accurately  
- avoid confusing correlation and causation  
- begin organizing a clear academic presentation  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (30 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “What Do These Results Mean?” (15 min)

Give a simple example:

> Students who sleep more have higher grades.

Ask:

- What does this mean?  
- Does sleep cause better grades?  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What can we say?
- What can we NOT say?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Share (15 min)

Write on board:

✔ What we can say  
✘ What we cannot say  

👉 Reinforce:
**correlation ≠ causation**

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (60 minutes)

## 3. Interpreting Results (Mini-Lesson) (25 min)

Teach:

Good interpretation includes:

- describing patterns  
- using careful language  
- avoiding strong claims  

---

### Example:

❌ “X causes Y”  
✅ “X is related to Y”

---

## 4. Common Mistakes (15 min)

Discuss:

- overgeneralizing  
- ignoring limitations  
- confusing variables  

---

### Ask:
> Why do people make these mistakes?

---

## 5. What Makes a Good Presentation? (20 min)

Teach:

Strong presentations:

- are clear  
- are organized  
- focus on key ideas  
- explain results simply  

---

### Structure:

1. Question  
2. Method  
3. Results  
4. Interpretation  

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (75 minutes)

## 6. Group Work: Interpreting Results (30 min)

Students work in groups:

- review their data  
- identify patterns  

---

### Prompt:

- What do your results show?  
- What trends do you see?  

---

## 7. Interpretation Practice (15 min)

Students write:

- one careful statement about results  

Example:
> “Our results suggest that…”

---

## 8. Presentation Planning (30 min)

Students begin organizing:

- slides  
- speaking roles  
- structure  

---

### Instructor circulates:

- checks clarity  
- supports interpretation  
- gives feedback  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (15 minutes)

## 9. Quick Share (10 min)

Groups share:

- one key result  
- one interpretation  

---

## 10. Exit Ticket (5 min)

Prompt:

> What is one thing you need to improve before your presentation?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Continue Inquiry Project  
- Finalize results + interpretation  
- Prepare presentation  

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (interpretation + presentation)  
- Interpretation worksheet  
- Presentation planning sheet  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Students may feel uncertain → normalize this  
- Emphasize:
  👉 “Your results do not need to be perfect”  
- Focus on:
  👉 clarity over complexity  
- Give targeted feedback during group work  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Interpreting results activity  
2. Writing careful claims  
3. Presentation planning  

👉 These prepare students for Week 11 presentations  

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

- shorter lecture  
- longer group work  
- frequent check-ins  

👉 workshop style, not lecture  

---

# 🎯 Role of This Week

This week:

- transforms data → understanding  
- prepares students for presentations  
- builds confidence before assessment
