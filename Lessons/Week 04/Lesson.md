# Week 4 Lesson Plan  
## Positive Emotions and Gratitude  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain the broaden-and-build theory  
- describe how positive emotions affect thinking and learning  
- define gratitude and explain its benefits  
- apply gratitude as a simple well-being practice  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: Emotional Recall (15 min)

Prompt:
> Think about a time when you felt really positive (happy, excited, calm).

Students write:

- What happened?  
- How did you feel?  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What did you do during that time?
- Did you think differently?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Share (10 min)

Ask:
- What do people do when they feel good?

Write on board:
- talk more  
- try new things  
- feel confident  

---

## 3. Quick Contrast (15 min)

Ask:
> What happens when you feel stressed or negative?

Students discuss:

👉 Narrow thinking vs open thinking

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 4. Broaden-and-Build Theory (30 min)

Explain simply:

**Positive emotions:**
- broaden thinking  
- help people explore, learn, connect  

**Over time:**
- build skills  
- build relationships  
- build resources  

---

### Visual Example:

Feeling:
- curious → try new things  
- relaxed → connect with others  

---

### Quick Check:
> What does “broaden” mean?  
> What does “build” mean?

---

## 5. Mini Application (15 min)

Ask:

> How could positive emotions help you in school?

Students discuss in pairs:
- studying  
- group work  
- presentations  

---

## 6. Introduce Gratitude (20 min)

Define:

**Gratitude = noticing and appreciating good things**

Examples:
- people  
- opportunities  
- small moments  

---

### Ask:
> Is gratitude natural or something we practice?

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Gratitude Practice (20 min)

Students write:

### Option A:
- 3 things they are grateful for  

### Option B:
- a short “gratitude letter”  

---

### Reflection:
- How did this feel?
- Was it easy or difficult?

---

## 8. Research Connection (15 min)

Connect to Week 3:

Ask:
> How do we know gratitude works?

Briefly review:

- research studies  
- participants  
- results  

---

## 9. Reading Discussion (15 min)

Students discuss:

- What does the reading say about gratitude?
- What are the benefits?

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Should people try to feel positive emotions more often?

Encourage:
- agreement/disagreement  
- examples  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is the broaden-and-build theory?  
> How does gratitude affect well-being?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Continue **Analytical Application**
- Reflect on gratitude practice
- Complete readings

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (theory + gratitude)  
- Gratitude writing sheet  
- Reading excerpts  
- Reflection prompts  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep tone **warm but not forced positivity**
- Acknowledge:
  👉 “You can’t be positive all the time”
- Emphasize:
  👉 positive emotions are helpful, not required  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Broaden-and-build explanation  
2. Gratitude practice  
3. Reflection discussion  

👉 These create both cognitive + emotional engagement

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- personal reflection  
- discussion  
- short explanation  
- writing  

👉 prevents fatigue in long class
