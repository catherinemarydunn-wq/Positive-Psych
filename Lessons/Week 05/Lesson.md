# Week 5 Lesson Plan  
## Stress and Self-Compassion  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain what stress is and how it affects thinking and behavior  
- identify common coping strategies  
- define self-compassion and its components  
- explain how self-compassion can support well-being  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “What Stresses You?” (15 min)

Prompt:
> What causes stress in your life right now?

Students write:

- school  
- work  
- relationships  
- time  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What are your top stressors?
- Are they similar?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Share (10 min)

Instructor lists common themes on board:

- deadlines  
- exams  
- group work  
- uncertainty  

---

## 3. Quick Reflection (15 min)

Ask:

> How do you usually respond to stress?

Students identify:

- helpful coping (exercise, talking)  
- unhelpful coping (avoidance, procrastination)

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 4. What is Stress? (Mini-Lecture) (25 min)

Explain simply:

**Stress = response to pressure or challenge**

Types:
- short-term (acute)  
- long-term (chronic)

---

### Effects of stress:

- thinking (focus ↓)  
- emotions (anxiety ↑)  
- behavior (avoidance)

---

### Quick Check:
> Is all stress bad?

Introduce idea:
👉 some stress can be helpful

---

## 5. Coping and Resilience (15 min)

Define:

**Coping = how we respond to stress**  
**Resilience = ability to recover**

---

### Ask:
> What helps people recover from stress?

---

## 6. Introduce Self-Compassion (25 min)

Define:

**Self-compassion = treating yourself with kindness when you struggle**

---

### Three components (simplified):

- Self-kindness  
- Common humanity  
- Mindfulness  

---

### Example:

Instead of:
> “I’m terrible at this”

Say:
> “This is hard, but I’m learning”

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Self-Compassion Reflection Activity (20 min)

Students reflect:

> Think of a recent challenge or mistake

Write:

- What happened?
- What did you say to yourself?

---

### Then rewrite:

> What would you say to a friend?

👉 Apply self-compassion

---

## 8. Research Connection (Neff & Germer) (15 min)

Students review:

- What was the study?
- What did participants do?
- What were the results?

---

### Key takeaway:

👉 self-compassion can be learned and improved

---

## 9. Pair Discussion (15 min)

Prompt:

> Is self-compassion helpful or difficult?

Discuss:
- benefits  
- challenges  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Is being kind to yourself always a good idea?

Encourage:
- different perspectives  
- examples  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is self-compassion?  
> How can it help with stress?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Analytical Application due**
- **Research Critique assigned (15%)**
- Review self-compassion concepts
- Continue readings

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (stress + self-compassion)  
- Reflection worksheet  
- Research worksheet  
- Discussion prompts  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Normalize stress → do not pathologize  
- Avoid “toxic positivity” tone  
- Emphasize:
  👉 self-compassion ≠ being lazy  
- Connect clearly to research (important for course coherence)

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Stress reflection  
2. Self-compassion rewrite activity  
3. Research connection  

👉 These create both cognitive + emotional engagement

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- personal reflection  
- discussion  
- mini-lecture  
- application  

👉 keeps engagement high in a heavy week
