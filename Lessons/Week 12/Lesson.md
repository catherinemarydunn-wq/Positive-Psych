# Week 12 Lesson Plan  
## Designing Interventions  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- define a positive psychology intervention  
- explain what makes an intervention effective  
- apply course concepts to real-world problems  
- begin designing their own intervention  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (35 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “What Actually Helps?” (15 min)

Prompt:

> Think about a time when something improved your well-being.

Students write:

- What helped?
- Why did it work?

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What kinds of things help people feel better?
- Are they simple or complex?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Share (10 min)

Write ideas on board:

- talking to someone  
- exercise  
- rest  
- planning  
- mindset  

---

## 3. Transition (10 min)

Ask:

> Can we design these ideas intentionally?

👉 Introduce idea of **interventions**

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (60 minutes)

## 4. What is an Intervention? (20 min)

Define:

**Intervention = a planned action designed to improve well-being**

---

### Examples:

- gratitude journaling  
- goal setting  
- stress management strategies  

---

## 5. What Makes an Intervention Effective? (20 min)

Teach:

Good interventions are:

- clear  
- realistic  
- based on research  
- appropriate for the situation  

---

### Ask:
> Why might an intervention fail?

---

## 6. Connect to Course Concepts (20 min)

Review:

- PERMA  
- SDT  
- stress + coping  
- strengths  
- gratitude  

---

### Key idea:

👉 Interventions use these concepts in real life

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (60 minutes)

## 7. Problem → Intervention Activity (25 min)

Give examples:

- stress in group work  
- lack of motivation  
- feeling isolated  

---

Students choose one and design:

- What is the problem?  
- What will you do?  
- Why will it work?  

---

## 8. Group Brainstorm (20 min)

Students begin:

- discussing ideas  
- forming initial plans  

---

## 9. Share + Feedback (15 min)

Groups share:

- one intervention idea  

Class gives feedback:

- Is it clear?  
- Is it realistic?  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Do interventions work for everyone?

Encourage:
- nuance  
- context  
- critical thinking  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is one intervention idea you are interested in?  
> Why do you think it will work?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Discussion Response #4 assigned**
- Begin planning Intervention Project  

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (interventions + examples)  
- Intervention design worksheet  
- Problem scenarios  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Emphasize:
  👉 simple interventions can be effective  
- Avoid:
  👉 “one solution fits all” thinking  
- Encourage:
  👉 connection to real student experiences  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Defining intervention  
2. Problem → solution activity  
3. Peer feedback  

👉 These move students into creation mode  

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

- discussion  
- brainstorming  
- group work  
- sharing  

👉 keeps engagement high  

---

# 🎯 Role of This Week

This week:

- integrates all course concepts  
- prepares students for final project  
- shifts students from learners → creators
