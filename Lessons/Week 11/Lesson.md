# Week 11 Lesson Plan  
## Inquiry Presentations  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** Adapted 4 A’s (Activate → Prepare → Present → Reflect)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- present research clearly and confidently  
- explain results and interpretations  
- listen actively and give constructive feedback  
- reflect on their own and others’ work  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (20 minutes)

## 1. Opening + Expectations (10 min)

Set tone:

- Presentations are about **communication, not perfection**
- Everyone is expected to:
  - listen  
  - engage  
  - give feedback  

---

## 2. Quick Warm-Up (10 min)

Ask:

> What makes a good presentation?

Students share ideas:

- clear  
- organized  
- confident  
- simple  

Write on board

---

# 🔵 PREPARE (20 minutes)

## 3. Final Preparation Time (20 min)

Groups:

- review slides  
- clarify roles  
- practice key points  

---

### Instructor role:

- check:
  - clarity of results  
  - careful interpretation  
  - timing  

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 PRESENT (90 minutes)

## 4. Inquiry Presentations (Main Activity)

### Structure:

Each group:

- 5–7 minutes presentation  
- 2–3 minutes questions  

---

### Suggested Timing (adjust as needed):

- 6–8 groups total  

---

### While listening:

Students complete **peer feedback sheet**

---

### Instructor role:

- keep time  
- ask 1–2 questions if needed  
- support quieter groups  

---

# 🔴 REFLECT (25 minutes)

## 5. Whole-Class Reflection (15 min)

Ask:

- What did you learn from other groups?  
- What was challenging?  
- What worked well?  

---

## 6. Individual Reflection (10 min)

Students write:

> What is one thing you did well?  
> What is one thing you would improve?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Inquiry Report due (if not already submitted)**  
- Begin thinking about **Intervention Project**

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Presentation rubric (optional)  
- Peer feedback forms  
- Timer  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep atmosphere **supportive, not high-pressure**  
- Avoid interrupting unless necessary  
- Ask gentle questions if groups struggle  

---

# 🔥 Core Goals of This Week

1. Build confidence  
2. Practice communication  
3. Reinforce research understanding  

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

- vary presenters  
- encourage audience participation  
- keep timing tight  

---

# 🎯 Role of This Week

This week:

- consolidates research skills  
- builds presentation confidence  
- prepares for final project work
