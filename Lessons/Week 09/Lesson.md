# Week 9 Lesson Plan  
## Methods, Simulation, and AI  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- describe the basic steps of a research study  
- explain what simulation-based research is  
- understand how AI can support academic work  
- apply research design ideas to their Inquiry Project  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (35 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “How Would You Study This?” (15 min)

Prompt:

> Does gratitude improve well-being?

Ask students:

- How could you study this?  

Students write ideas individually

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What would you measure?
- Who would you study?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Share (10 min)

Instructor lists ideas:

- surveys  
- experiments  
- interviews  

---

## 3. Transition (10 min)

Explain:

👉 These are all examples of **research design**

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (70 minutes)

## 4. Designing a Simple Study (30 min)

Introduce key steps:

- Research question  
- Variables (what is measured)  
- Participants  
- Method  

---

### Simple Example:

Question:
> Does sleep affect focus?

Variables:
- sleep (hours)  
- focus (score)

---

### Quick Check:
> What is the variable?  
> What is being measured?

---

## 5. What is Simulation? (20 min)

Explain simply:

**Simulation = creating a model of data instead of collecting real data**

---

### Example:

Instead of surveying 100 people:
👉 simulate responses  

---

### Key idea:

- faster  
- easier  
- but not perfect  

---

## 6. Introduction to AI in Research (20 min)

Explain:

AI can help with:

- generating ideas  
- organizing data  
- writing drafts  

---

### But:

- must be used responsibly  
- must be checked  
- must be acknowledged  

---

### Ask:
> What are the risks of using AI?

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Mini Research Design Activity (20 min)

In pairs/groups:

Students choose a topic:

- stress  
- sleep  
- gratitude  
- motivation  

---

### Task:

Create a simple study:

- Question  
- Variables  
- Method  

---

## 8. Simulation Practice (15 min)

Students imagine:

- What would the data look like?
- What results might you get?

---

## 9. Inquiry Project Work Time (15 min)

Students:

- form groups (if not done)  
- begin planning proposal  
- discuss ideas  

Instructor circulates and supports

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Reflection (10 min)

Prompt:

> What makes a study strong?

Students share ideas:
- clear question  
- good method  
- realistic data  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is one idea for your Inquiry Project?  
> How could you study it?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Discussion Response #3 assigned**
- Begin Inquiry Project  
- Develop research question  

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (research design + AI)  
- Study design worksheet  
- Simulation worksheet  
- AI guidelines sheet  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep everything **simple and concrete**  
- Avoid technical language  
- Emphasize:
  👉 “You are not doing perfect research—you are learning the process”  
- AI discussion should be clear but not alarmist  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Study design activity  
2. Simulation explanation  
3. Inquiry project start  

👉 These reduce anxiety and build confidence  

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- discussion  
- mini-lecture  
- group work  
- planning  

👉 keeps engagement high  

---

This week:

- builds research confidence  
- introduces AI responsibly  
- transitions into inquiry work  
- supports independent thinking
