# Week 6 Lesson Plan  
## Strengths and Grit  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain what character strengths are  
- identify their own strengths  
- describe the concept of grit  
- evaluate the strengths and limitations of grit  
- connect course ideas to their own experiences  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “What Are You Good At?” (15 min)

Prompt:

> What are your strengths?

Students write:

- skills  
- personality traits  
- things they do well  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What did you write?
- Was it easy or difficult?

👉 Many students will say it’s difficult → important moment

---

## 2. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Ask:

- Why is it hard to talk about strengths?
- What do people usually focus on instead?

Write on board:
- weaknesses  
- mistakes  
- grades  

---

## 3. Transition (15 min)

Ask:

> What if focusing on strengths could improve well-being?

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 4. Character Strengths (Mini-Lecture) (30 min)

Explain:

**Character strengths = positive traits in how we think, feel, and act**

Examples:
- kindness  
- curiosity  
- perseverance  
- honesty  

---

### Key idea:

👉 Everyone has strengths  
👉 Using strengths → better well-being  

---

### Quick Activity:

Students choose 2 strengths they think they have

---

## 5. Applying Strengths (15 min)

Ask:

> How could you use your strengths in school?

Examples:
- curiosity → better learning  
- kindness → group work  
- perseverance → assignments  

---

## 6. Introduce Grit (20 min)

Define:

**Grit = persistence + passion over time**

Explain simply:
- working hard  
- not giving up  

---

### Ask:

> Is grit always a good thing?

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Strengths Application Activity (20 min)

Students choose one strength:

Fill in:

- My strength: __________________  
- How I use it now: __________________  
- How I could use it more: __________________  

---

## 8. Grit Discussion + Critique (30 min)

Students read/discuss:

- What does the reading say about grit?
- What are the benefits?
- What are the problems?

---

### Guide discussion toward:

- effort vs opportunity  
- individual vs structural factors  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 9. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Is success only about effort?

Encourage:
- different perspectives  
- real examples  

---

## 10. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is one strength you have?  
> How can you use it more?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Continue **Research Critique**
- Reflect on strengths
- Complete readings

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (strengths + grit)  
- Strengths worksheet  
- Reading excerpts  
- Discussion prompts  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Students often struggle to identify strengths → normalize this  
- Avoid “you can do anything” messaging  
- Emphasize:
  👉 strengths are useful, but context matters  
- Grit critique is important → don’t skip it  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Strength identification  
2. Strengths application activity  
3. Grit critique discussion  

👉 These balance empowerment + critical thinking

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- reflection  
- discussion  
- mini-lecture  
- application  

👉 keeps engagement high

---

# 🎯 Pedagogical Role of This Week

This week:

- builds confidence (strengths)  
- introduces critique (grit limits)  
- prepares students for:
  👉 research critique  
  👉 later intervention design
