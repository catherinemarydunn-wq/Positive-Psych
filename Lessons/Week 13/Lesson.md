# Week 13 Lesson Plan  
## Intervention Project Workshop  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** Adapted 4 A’s (Activate → Focus → Build → Refine)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- refine their intervention design  
- clearly explain how their intervention works  
- connect their intervention to course concepts  
- improve the clarity and structure of their presentation  

---

## 🟢 ACTIVATE (20 minutes)

### 1. Quick Reset: What Makes a Strong Intervention? (10 min)

**Prompt:**
> What makes an intervention effective?

Students brainstorm in pairs, then share with the class.

Instructor writes key ideas on the board:
- clear  
- realistic  
- specific  
- based on course concepts  
- easy to follow  

---

### 2. Example Comparison (10 min)

Present two examples:

**Weak:**
> “Students should be more positive”

**Strong:**
> “Students write 3 positive things daily for 1 week”

**Discussion:**
- Which is better?
- Why?

👉 Emphasize specificity and action

---

## 🔵 FOCUS (25 minutes)

### 3. Clarify Project Expectations (15 min)

Review what the Intervention Project must include:

- clear problem  
- specific intervention (what people do)  
- explanation (why it works)  
- connection to course concepts (e.g., PERMA, SDT, stress, strengths, gratitude)

Encourage students to check their current progress.

---

### 4. Common Issues to Avoid (10 min)

Highlight typical problems:

- too vague  
- not realistic  
- unclear steps  
- weak connection to theory  
- overly complex ideas  

👉 Encourage students to simplify and clarify

---

## ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

## 🟡 BUILD (75 minutes)

### 5. Group Work: Intervention Development (45 min)

Students work in their groups to:

- refine their intervention  
- clarify steps  
- strengthen connection to course concepts  

**Instructor role:**
- circulate and support  
- ask guiding questions:
  - What exactly will people do?
  - Why will this work?
  - Is this realistic?
  - Can you simplify this?

---

### 6. Midpoint Check-In (15 min)

Each group shares:

- their intervention idea (1–2 minutes)

Class provides quick feedback:
- Is it clear?
- Is it realistic?
- Does it connect to course concepts?

---

### 7. Continue Group Work (15 min)

Students revise their intervention based on feedback.

---

## 🔴 REFINE (25 minutes)

### 8. Peer Feedback (15 min)

Groups exchange their work.

Students use the feedback sheet to evaluate:

- clarity  
- realism  
- connection to course concepts  

---

### 9. Individual Reflection (10 min)

Students write:

> What is one thing you improved today?  
> What still needs work?

---

## 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Continue working on the Intervention Project  
- Finalize presentation and report  
- Prepare for Week 14 presentations  

---

## 🎯 Materials Needed

- Intervention design worksheet (Week 12)  
- Peer feedback sheet  
- Slides (optional, minimal use)

---

## 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep the tone supportive and practical  
- Focus on:
  - clarity over complexity  
  - realistic, doable interventions  
- Encourage students to:
  - simplify their ideas  
  - use clear, concrete language  

---

## 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Weak vs strong example  
2. Midpoint check-in  
3. Peer feedback  

👉 These significantly improve final project quality

---

## ⚡ Energy Strategy

- short whole-class moments  
- extended group work  
- structured peer interaction  

👉 maintains engagement in a workshop format

---

## 🎯 Role of This Week

This week:

- strengthens intervention design  
- improves clarity and communication  
- prepares students for final presentations
