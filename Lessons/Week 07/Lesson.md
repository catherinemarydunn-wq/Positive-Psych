# Week 7 Lesson Plan  
## Self-Determination Theory (Motivation and Well-Being)  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain the three basic psychological needs in Self-Determination Theory  
- describe how motivation affects learning and well-being  
- apply SDT to their own academic experiences  
- discuss how motivation differs across situations  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: Motivation Check (15 min)

Prompt:

> Think about a class or activity you feel motivated to do.

Write:

- What is it?  
- Why do you want to do it?  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- Why are you motivated?
- Is it internal or external?

---

## 2. Contrast Activity (15 min)

Ask:

> Think of something you do NOT feel motivated to do.

Students reflect:

- Why not?
- What is missing?

---

## 3. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Write on board:

What motivates people?

Students may say:
- interest  
- rewards  
- pressure  
- goals  

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 4. Introduce Self-Determination Theory (25 min)

Explain simply:

Motivation improves when three needs are met:

- Autonomy → feeling of choice  
- Competence → feeling capable  
- Relatedness → feeling connected  

---

### Use examples:

Autonomy:
- choosing your topic  

Competence:
- understanding the material  

Relatedness:
- feeling supported in class  

---

### Quick Check:
> Which is most important?

---

## 5. Deepen Understanding (20 min)

Explain:

When these needs are met:
- motivation increases  
- well-being improves  

When they are not:
- motivation decreases  

---

### Ask:
> Have you experienced this?

---

## 6. Link to Previous Weeks (20 min)

Connect:

- strengths → competence  
- gratitude → relatedness  
- stress → lack of control (autonomy)

👉 This reinforces course coherence

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. SDT Self-Assessment Activity (20 min)

Students evaluate:

| Need | High / Medium / Low | Example |
|------|--------------------|--------|
| Autonomy | | |
| Competence | | |
| Relatedness | | |

---

### Reflection:
- Which need is strongest?
- Which is weakest?

---

## 8. Scenario Activity (15 min)

Give situations:

- group project  
- difficult class  
- boring lecture  

Students identify:
- which needs are met/not met  

---

## 9. Pair Discussion (15 min)

Prompt:

> How can you increase motivation in school?

Students must:
- use SDT vocabulary  
- give examples  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Is motivation something you control, or something influenced by your environment?

Encourage:
- debate  
- examples  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What are the three needs in Self-Determination Theory?  
> Which one is most important for you?

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep language simple → SDT can feel abstract  
- Use MANY real-life examples  
- Reinforce connections to earlier weeks  
- Encourage honest reflection (not “perfect student” answers)

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Motivation contrast activity  
2. SDT explanation (simple + clear)  
3. Self-assessment  
4. Scenario analysis  

👉 These build both understanding + application

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- personal reflection  
- discussion  
- mini-lecture  
- application  

👉 prevents fatigue in a long class
