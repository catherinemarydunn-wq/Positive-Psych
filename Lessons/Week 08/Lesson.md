# Week 8 Lesson Plan  
## Goals and Flow  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain what makes a good goal  
- describe the concept of flow  
- identify conditions that support focus and engagement  
- apply goal-setting strategies to their own academic work  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “When Do You Focus Best?” (15 min)

Prompt:

> Think about a time when you were really focused.

Write:

- What were you doing?  
- Why were you focused?  

---

### Pair Discussion:
- What helped you focus?
- What made it easier or harder?

---

## 2. Whole-Class Brainstorm (10 min)

Ask:

> What helps people focus?

Write on board:

- clear goals  
- interest  
- quiet environment  
- challenge  

---

## 3. Transition (15 min)

Ask:

> What makes a goal effective?

Students suggest ideas:
- clear  
- realistic  
- meaningful  

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 4. Goal Setting (30 min)

Teach:

**Effective goals are:**
- specific  
- realistic  
- meaningful  

---

### Example:

❌ “Study more”  
✅ “Study biology for 30 minutes every day”

---

### Quick Check:
> Which goal is better? Why?

---

## 5. Self-Regulation (15 min)

Define:

**Self-regulation = managing your time, attention, and effort**

Examples:
- planning  
- avoiding distractions  
- staying on task  

---

### Ask:
> What makes self-regulation difficult?

---

## 6. Introduce Flow (20 min)

Define:

**Flow = a state of deep focus and engagement**

---

### Conditions for flow:

- clear goal  
- challenge matches skill  
- no distractions  

---

### Example:

- gaming  
- sports  
- writing  

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Goal Setting Activity (20 min)

Students create a goal:

- specific  
- realistic  
- meaningful  

---

### Write:

My goal:

________________________________________

---

Steps to achieve it:

________________________________________

---

## 8. Flow Reflection Activity (15 min)

Students reflect:

- When do I experience flow?
- What conditions help me focus?

---

## 9. Inquiry Project Setup (15 min)

Explain:

- project overview  
- group formation  
- proposal checkpoint  

---

Students:

- form groups  
- discuss possible ideas  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Is motivation enough for success?

Encourage:
- examples  
- connections to goals and flow  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is one goal you will work on this week?  
> What will help you stay focused?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Work on Inquiry Project proposal  
- Apply goal-setting strategies  
- Complete readings  

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (goals + flow)  
- Goal-setting worksheet  
- Flow reflection sheet  
- Inquiry project instructions  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- Keep this week practical → avoid too much theory  
- Emphasize:
  👉 small, realistic goals  
- Flow is intuitive → use examples  
- This is a transition week → connect to project work  

---

# 🔥 Core Learning Moments

1. Goal-setting activity  
2. Flow explanation  
3. Inquiry project launch  

👉 These move students from theory → action  

---

# ⚡ Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- reflection  
- discussion  
- mini-lecture  
- planning  

👉 keeps engagement high

---

# 🎯 Role of This Week

This week:

- moves students into action  
- prepares for inquiry work  
- builds self-regulation skills
