# Week 2 Lesson Plan  
## PERMA and Cultural Perspectives  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain the PERMA model of well-being  
- describe alternative cultural perspectives on well-being  
- compare different models of a “good life”  
- express and support their opinions in discussion  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (40 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: Revisiting the “Good Life” (15 min)

Prompt:
> Last week, we asked: What makes a good life?

Now ask:
> Would you change your answer?

### Steps:
- Students write (3–5 min)
- Pair discussion
- Whole-class share

👉 Instructor writes key ideas on board

---

## 2. Ranking Activity: What Matters Most? (25 min)

Give students a list:

- happiness  
- success  
- relationships  
- meaning  
- health  
- money  

### Task:
- Rank top 3 individually  
- Compare in pairs  
- Whole class discussion  

👉 Transition:
“Psychologists also try to answer this question…”

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (65 minutes)

## 3. Introduce PERMA (25 min)

Explain each element:

- Positive Emotion  
- Engagement  
- Relationships  
- Meaning  
- Accomplishment  

### Activity:
After each element, ask:

> “Can you give an example?”

Keep it interactive—not lecture-heavy

---

## 4. Quick Check (10 min)

Ask:

- Which PERMA element is most important?  
- Which is least important?  

Students move to different sides of the room OR discuss in pairs

---

## 5. Transition to Critique (10 min)

Ask:

> Does this model work for everyone?

Write answers on board

---

## 6. Cultural Perspectives Mini-Lesson (20 min)

Introduce:

### Indigenous frameworks
- Medicine Wheel (balance)
- Emotional, physical, mental, spiritual

### Key idea:
👉 well-being = balance, not just feeling good

Keep it simple and respectful

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (50 minutes)

## 7. Jigsaw Reading (30 min)

Groups:

- Group A → PERMA reading  
- Group B → Indigenous teachings  
- Group C → Joshanloo  

### Task:
- Identify 2–3 key ideas  
- Prepare to explain  

Then:
- mix groups  
- students teach each other  

---

## 8. Comparison Task (20 min)

Students complete:

| Model | Focus | Key Idea |
|------|------|---------|
| PERMA | | |
| Indigenous | | |
| Joshanloo | | |

### Guiding questions:
- What is similar?
- What is different?
- Which is more realistic?

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 9. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Prompt:

> Is happiness the most important goal in life?

Students must:
- give opinion  
- give reason  
- respond to someone else  

---

## 10. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> Compare PERMA with another model of well-being.  
> Which do you agree with more, and why?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Discussion Response #1 due**
- Revise if needed
- Bring one question about cultural perspectives

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (PERMA + cultural frameworks)  
- Ranking list (printed or projected)  
- Jigsaw readings  
- Comparison chart  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- This is a **concept-heavy week** → slow down  
- Don’t over-explain PERMA → let students generate examples  
- Be thoughtful with Indigenous content → emphasize respect + perspective, not simplification  
- The comparison task is the **core learning moment**

---

# 🔥 Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- writing  
- pair work  
- movement (ranking / positioning)  
- discussion  

👉 Prevents fatigue in a 3-hour class
