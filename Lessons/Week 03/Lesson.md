# Week 3 Lesson Plan  
## Evaluating Research  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain what empirical research is  
- identify key parts of a research study  
- distinguish between correlation and causation  
- evaluate a simple research study using a checklist  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (35 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “Can We Trust This?” (15 min)

Show 2–3 claims:

- “People who drink coffee live longer.”  
- “Students who study more get better grades.”  
- “Exercise improves mood.”  

### Ask:
> Do you believe this? Why or why not?

Students:
- vote (yes/no/not sure)
- discuss in pairs

---

## 2. Whole-Class Discussion (10 min)

Ask:
- How do we know if something is true?
- What kind of evidence is strong?

Write answers on board:
- data  
- experiments  
- personal experience  

---

## 3. Introduce Key Term (10 min)

### Empirical Research

Explain:
> Research based on **data and observation**, not just opinion

Give simple example:
- survey
- experiment

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (70 minutes)

## 4. How Research Works (Mini-Lecture) (25 min)

Introduce basic structure:

- Research question  
- Participants  
- Method  
- Results  

Use VERY simple example:

> “Does gratitude journaling improve mood?”

Check understanding often:
- What is the question?
- What is measured?

---

## 5. Correlation vs. Causation (25 min)

### Teach clearly:

**Correlation**
- two things happen together  

**Causation**
- one thing causes another  

---

### Use examples:

- Ice cream sales ↑ → crime ↑  
👉 correlated, not causal  

---

### Guided practice:

Ask:
> Does studying cause better grades?

Students discuss:
- maybe yes  
- maybe other factors (motivation, time)

---

## 6. Quick Practice (20 min)

Give examples:

Students decide:
- correlation  
- causation  

Discuss answers as class

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (55 minutes)

## 7. Reading Task (Simplified Article) (25 min)

Students read:

👉 simplified empirical article

### Task:
Identify:

- What is the research question?  
- What did they do?  
- What did they find?  

---

## 8. Research Evaluation Checklist (15 min)

Introduce simple checklist:

- What is the question?  
- How was the study done?  
- Are there limitations?  
- Correlation or causation?  

---

## 9. Pair Evaluation (15 min)

Students use checklist to evaluate the article

Then share:

- one strength  
- one limitation  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (20 minutes)

## 10. Whole-Class Reflection (10 min)

Prompt:

> Can we trust this study?

Students must:
- give opinion  
- use evidence  

---

## 11. Exit Ticket (10 min)

Prompt:

> What is the difference between correlation and causation?  
> Why is this important?

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- **Analytical Application assigned (15%)**
- Review research checklist
- Re-read article if needed

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (research basics + correlation)  
- Example claims  
- Simplified article  
- Evaluation checklist  

---

# 💡 Instructor Notes

- This is a **threshold concept week** → go slow  
- Students will confuse correlation/causation → repeat + examples  
- Keep language simple  
- Use MANY examples  

---

# 🔥 Energy Strategy

Alternate:

- discussion  
- examples  
- quick decisions  
- reading  

👉 Avoid long lecture blocks

---

# 🎯 Core Learning Moment

👉 The **evaluation checklist activity**

This is what prepares them for:
- Analytical Application  
- future weeks
