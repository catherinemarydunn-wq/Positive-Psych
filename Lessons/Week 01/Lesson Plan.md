# Week 1 Lesson Plan  
## GPSE 100 – Introduction to Positive Psychology  
**Duration:** 3 hours (with 15-minute break)  
**Model:** 4 A’s (Activate → Acquire → Apply → Assess)

---

## Learning Objectives (Student-Friendly)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

- explain the difference between happiness and flourishing  
- describe what positive psychology is  
- use basic course vocabulary in discussion  
- share ideas about well-being in a group  

---

# 🟢 ACTIVATE (45 minutes)

## 1. Warm-Up: “What Makes a Good Life?” (15 min)

**Activity: Think–Pair–Share**

Prompt:
> What does a “good life” mean to you?

Steps:
- Students write for 2–3 minutes  
- Discuss with a partner  
- Share ideas with class  

**Teacher role:**
- Write key words on board (e.g., money, family, success, happiness)

---

## 2. Class Poll + Discussion (15 min)

Ask:
- Is happiness the most important goal in life?
- Yes / No / Not sure

Students vote (hands, Mentimeter, or show of hands)

Follow-up:
- Why?
- What is missing from “happiness”?

---

## 3. Vocabulary Activation (15 min)

Introduce key terms:

- happiness  
- well-being  
- flourishing  

**Activity: Matching or guessing meanings**

Students:
- match definitions  
- create example sentences  

---

# 🔵 ACQUIRE (60 minutes)

## 4. Mini Lecture: What is Positive Psychology? (20 min)

Cover:
- traditional psychology (focus on problems)
- positive psychology (focus on strengths + well-being)
- shift from “fixing” → “building”

Keep interactive:
- ask quick questions throughout
- check understanding frequently

---

## 5. Happiness vs. Flourishing (20 min)

Explain:

- Happiness = feeling good  
- Flourishing = functioning well + meaning + growth  

Use examples:
- “happy but lost” vs. “challenged but growing”

**Quick pair discussion:**
> Can someone flourish without feeling happy all the time?

---

## 6. Introduce Course Structure (20 min)

Brief overview:

- topics  
- major assignments  
- how AI will be used  
- expectations  

Keep it light:
- don’t overwhelm  
- emphasize support + growth  

---

# ☕ BREAK (15 minutes)

---

# 🟡 APPLY (45 minutes)

## 7. Reading Jigsaw (Held vs. Hart) (30 min)

Split class:

- Group A → Hart (intro ideas)
- Group B → Held (critique)

Task:
- identify 2–3 key ideas  
- prepare to explain to others  

Then:
- re-group and teach each other  

---

## 8. Whole-Class Discussion (15 min)

Guiding question:

> Should psychology focus more on problems or strengths?

Encourage:
- agreement/disagreement  
- use of new vocabulary  

---

# 🔴 ASSESS (15 minutes)

## 9. Exit Ticket (Individual Writing)

Prompt:

> What is the difference between happiness and flourishing?  
> Which do you think is more important, and why?

Collect or review quickly

---

# 🧠 Homework / Follow-Up

- Begin **Discussion Response #1**
- Complete readings (Hart + Held)
- Bring one question about the readings next class

---

# 🎯 Materials Needed

- Slides (simple, visual)  
- Whiteboard or shared doc  
- Poll tool (optional)  
- Short reading excerpts
