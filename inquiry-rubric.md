# Mini Inquiry Assessment Rubric

## Overview

The Mini Inquiry (25% of the course grade) has four assessable components. The weighting below is a starting point; instructors adjust based on their section and which individual accountability mechanisms they use.

| Component | Suggested weight | Individual or group | What it assesses |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------------|-----------------| 
| Report | 10% | Group | Research reasoning, source-grounded argumentation, clarity of writing |
| Presentation + Q&A | 5% | Group (presentation) + individual (Q&A) | Communication, evidence use, oral defence of design decisions |
| Reading test | 5% | Individual | Source verification, error detection, inferential evaluation |
| LLM interaction portfolio | 5% | Individual | Quality of LLM interactions, range of language use, evidence of iteration |

## Report rubric

The report has five sections. Method is LLM-drafted and student-revised. Results, Discussion, and Limitations are student-written with LLM assistance.

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Research question | Clear, specific, operationalisable. Connected to course content and a characteristic that varies. |
| Method | Accurate description of design decisions (participant design, instrument, effect model, priors, pipeline validation). Specifies LLM, seed, sample size. |
| Results | Accurate description of posteriors, credible intervals, and plots. Uses disciplinary terminology correctly. Appropriate hedging. |
| Discussion | Interprets patterns in relation to the research question. Identifies which assumptions drive the result. Considers what would change with real participants. |
| Limitations | Addresses the four mandatory limitations (effect model vs. psychology, prior influence, LLM training data, simulation circularity). Goes beyond the minimum where possible. |

## Presentation + Q&A rubric

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Structure | Follows the Quarto template. Slides are readable and organised. |
| Evidence use | Claims supported by plots and posterior summaries. Plots described accurately. |
| Design defence | Each group member can explain and justify the decisions their role owned. |
| Communication | Clear delivery. Appropriate register. Responds to Q&A substantively. |

During Q&A, the instructor directs at least one question to each group member about the decisions their role owned. The quality of individual responses contributes to individual assessment.

## Notes for instructors

- The suggested 10/5/5/5 split ensures that 15% of the 25% is individually assessed (reading test + portfolio), with additional individual evidence from Q&A responses.
- Instructors who add an individual reflection (see the methodology guide's individual accountability section) may redistribute weight accordingly.
- The report's language quality is assessed through the criteria above (disciplinary terminology, hedging, clarity), not as a separate "language" category. Language is the medium, not a bolt-on.
