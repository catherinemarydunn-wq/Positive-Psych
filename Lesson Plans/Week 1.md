# GPSE 100 Week 1 Lesson Plan: What Is Well-Being?

**Context:** 1 x 3-hour class (with break), B2 learners  
**Week 1 of 14**

---

## Learning Objectives (Week 1)

By the end of this class, students will be able to:

1. Explain the basic idea of **positive psychology**  
2. Distinguish between **happiness and flourishing**  
3. Use key academic vocabulary (e.g., well-being, flourishing)  
4. Express personal ideas about well-being using academic language  

---

## Weekly Thread — The Language of Well-Being

This course focuses on:
- Understanding **what helps people thrive**
- Learning to **express ideas clearly in English**
- Applying ideas to **real academic and life situations**

Each class follows a rhythm:
> Learn → Discuss → Apply → Reflect  

---

# Segment A: Introduction + Activation (0:00–0:45)

## 0:00–0:10 | Welcome + Course Framing  
**Format:** Instructor-led  

- Instructor intro  
- Frame the course:

> “This course is about understanding what helps people succeed—not just academically, but in life. You will learn ideas, but more importantly, you will apply them to your own experience.”

- Set expectations:
  - Participation matters  
  - Focus on **ideas + language**

---

## 0:10–0:30 | Think–Pair–Share: What Is a Good Life?  
**Format:** Individual → Pair → Whole class  

**Prompt (on slide):**
- What does a “good life” mean?  
- What makes someone successful or happy?  

**Steps:**
- Think (3 min, jot notes)  
- Pair (5 min each)  
- Whole class share  

Instructor builds a **word cloud or list** on board (money, family, health, success, etc.)

---

## 0:30–0:45 | Vocabulary Pre-Teaching  
**Format:** Instructor-led + interactive  

**Key terms:**
- Happiness  
- Well-being  
- Flourishing  

For each:
1. Give simple definition  
2. Give example  
3. Ask quick question  

**Check:**
- “Which word is the most complete idea? Why?”  

---

# Segment B: Input + Guided Reading (0:45–1:40)

## 0:45–1:05 | Mini-Lecture: What Is Positive Psychology?  
**Format:** Instructor-led  

- Traditional psychology = problems  
- Positive psychology = strengths + well-being  

**Key idea:**
> Flourishing = living well over time, not just feeling good  

---

## 1:05–1:25 | Guided Reading (Hart, 2021)  
**Format:** Individual → Pair  

**Task:**
Students read excerpt and answer:

- What is positive psychology?  
- What is flourishing?  

**Support:**
- Highlight key sentences  
- Use guiding questions  

---

## 1:25–1:40 | Pair Discussion + Whole Class Share  

**Questions:**
- What is the main idea?  
- What surprised you?  

Instructor clarifies misunderstandings  

---

## Break (1:40–1:55)

---

# Segment C: Critical Thinking + Application (1:55–2:40)

## 1:55–2:10 | Critical Perspective (Held, 2004)  
**Format:** Instructor input + discussion  

**Key idea:**
- Positive psychology has limitations  
- Culture matters  

**Prompt:**
- Is happiness the same in all cultures?  

Quick pair discussion → share  

---

## 2:10–2:25 | Application Activity  

**Prompt:**
Think about your life:
- What helps you feel good?  
- What helps you succeed?  

Students write short notes  

---

## 2:25–2:40 | Small Group Discussion  

**Task:**
In groups of 3:
- Share your ideas  
- Find:
  - 2 similarities  
  - 1 difference  

Report back  

---

# Segment D: Writing + Reflection (2:40–3:00)

## 2:40–2:50 | Discussion Response #1 Setup  

**Prompt:**
> “What is well-being? Is it the same in all cultures?”  

**Sentence frames (on slide):**
- “Well-being can be defined as…”  
- “According to the reading…”  
- “In my experience…”  
- “However, this idea may not apply to all cultures because…”  

---

## 2:50–3:00 | Quick Write (In-Class Start)

Students begin writing (8–10 min)

Instructor circulates and supports  

---

# Closing (Last 2–3 minutes)

- Review key ideas:
  - Positive psychology  
  - Happiness vs. flourishing  
- Reinforce:
> “In this course, we don’t just learn ideas—we apply them.”

- Reminder:
  - Finish Discussion Response #1 for Week 2
