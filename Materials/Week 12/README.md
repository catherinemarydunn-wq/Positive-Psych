# Week 12 – Designing Interventions

## Overview

This week focuses on positive psychology interventions and how they can be designed to support well-being. Students will explore what makes an intervention effective and consider how psychological concepts can be applied in real-world contexts.

The week emphasizes both practical application and critical thinking about what works and why.

---

## Learning Focus

By the end of this week, students should be able to:

- Define a positive psychology intervention  
- Explain what makes an intervention effective  
- Apply course concepts to design an intervention  
- Think critically about the limits of interventions

## Note to Students

This week focuses on applying what you have learned throughout the course. As you read, think about how different concepts—such as motivation, strengths, gratitude, and self-compassion—can be used to design interventions.

Also consider the limits of these approaches. Not all interventions work in the same way for all people or in all contexts.
