# Week 3 – Evaluating Research

## Overview

This week introduces students to empirical research in positive psychology. Students learn how to read and understand a research study, with a focus on key concepts such as variables, study design, and the difference between correlation and causation.

Students will apply these skills by analyzing a simplified research article.

---

## Learning Focus

By the end of this week, students should be able to:

- Identify key parts of a research study (e.g., purpose, method, results)  
- Distinguish between correlation and causation  
- Understand basic research terminology (e.g., variables, participants)  
- Apply a structured approach to reading academic research

## Note to Students

This week focuses on developing your research skills. You are not expected to understand every detail of the study. Instead, focus on understanding the purpose of the research, how it was conducted, and what the results mean.

Use the “How to Read a Research Study” guide to help you work through the sample article.
