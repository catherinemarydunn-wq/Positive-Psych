# Using AI in Academic Work: Guidelines and Responsibilities  
*(Instructional Guide for Student Inquiry Projects)*

---

## ⚠️ Note to Students

In this course, you are allowed to use AI tools to support your learning. However, you are responsible for understanding, evaluating, and accurately representing any work produced with AI.

This guide explains how to use AI appropriately in your inquiry project and other assignments.

---

## Introduction

Artificial intelligence (AI) tools can support many aspects of academic work, including generating ideas, organizing information, and drafting text. In this course, AI is used as a *tool* to help you complete your inquiry project.

However, using AI does not replace your responsibility as a student. You are expected to make decisions, evaluate outputs, and understand the work you submit.

---

## What You Are Responsible For

Even when you use AI, you are responsible for:

- the accuracy of your content  
- the quality of your explanations  
- the decisions you make in your project  
- the final work you submit  

This means that you must understand what the AI produces and be able to explain it in your own words.

---

## Appropriate Uses of AI

In this course, AI can be used to support your work in several ways.

You may use AI to:

- generate ideas for research questions  
- help design survey questions  
- assist with organizing your research plan  
- generate or revise written drafts  
- help explain complex concepts  
- support data analysis and interpretation  

In your inquiry project, AI may also be used to generate simulated data and assist with analysis.

---

## Inappropriate Uses of AI

Some uses of AI are not acceptable.

You should not:

- submit AI-generated work without reviewing it  
- rely on AI to make all decisions in your project  
- copy AI output without understanding it  
- present AI-generated ideas as your own without acknowledgment  
- use AI to avoid learning or thinking  

Using AI in these ways may result in academic integrity concerns.

---

## Evaluating AI Output

AI tools can produce incorrect or misleading information. For this reason, it is important to evaluate all AI-generated content.

When using AI, you should ask:

- Is this information accurate?  
- Does it match what I have learned in class?  
- Is anything unclear or incorrect?  
- Do I agree with this explanation?  

If something seems wrong or confusing, you should revise it or ask for clarification.

---

## Using AI in the Inquiry Project

In the inquiry project, AI is used to support the research process. However, your role is to make the key decisions.

You are responsible for:

- choosing your research question  
- defining your variables  
- deciding how your model works  
- interpreting your results  
- explaining your conclusions and limitations  

AI can assist with execution, but it does not replace your thinking.

---

## Transparency and Acknowledgment

When you use AI, you should be transparent about it.

In your project, you may include a brief statement such as:

> “AI tools were used to support drafting, data generation, and analysis. All outputs were reviewed and revised by the student.”

This shows that you understand your role in the process.

---

## Conclusion

AI can be a valuable tool for learning and research. When used responsibly, it can support your understanding and help you complete complex tasks.

However, your responsibility as a student remains the same. You must think critically, evaluate information, and take ownership of your work. The goal of this course is not just to produce results, but to understand how those results are created and what they mean.
