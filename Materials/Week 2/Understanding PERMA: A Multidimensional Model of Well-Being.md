# Understanding PERMA: A Multidimensional Model of Well-Being  
*(Adapted from Seligman, 2011)*

## Introduction

In recent decades, psychology has expanded its focus beyond the study of mental illness to include the study of well-being. Rather than asking only how people recover from difficulties, researchers in positive psychology ask how people can live meaningful, satisfying, and productive lives. One of the most influential frameworks developed within this field is the PERMA model, proposed by Martin Seligman.

The PERMA model suggests that well-being is not a single experience, such as happiness, but a combination of several distinct elements. These elements—Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment—represent different ways in which people experience and build a good life. Each element contributes to well-being in its own way, and together they form a more complete understanding of human flourishing.

---

## Rethinking Well-Being

Traditional approaches to well-being often focused on happiness as the main goal of life. In this view, well-being is defined by the presence of positive emotions and the absence of negative ones. However, this approach has limitations. People may feel happy in the moment but still experience a lack of purpose, connection, or achievement.

The PERMA model offers a broader perspective. It recognizes that well-being includes both how people feel and how they function in their lives. This means that well-being is not only about pleasure, but also about involvement, relationships, purpose, and progress.

Importantly, each element of PERMA is considered valuable in itself. People may pursue these elements for different reasons, and not all individuals will prioritize them in the same way.

---

## Positive Emotion

Positive emotion refers to experiences such as joy, gratitude, hope, and contentment. These emotions are important because they contribute to life satisfaction and can improve overall mood. They also make daily experiences more enjoyable and rewarding.

However, the PERMA model does not suggest that people should feel positive all the time. Negative emotions are a normal and necessary part of life. Instead, positive emotion is understood as one important component of well-being, rather than its only goal.

---

## Engagement

Engagement involves being deeply involved in an activity. When individuals are fully engaged, they concentrate intensely and often lose awareness of time. This experience is commonly described as “flow.”

Flow tends to occur when there is a balance between challenge and skill. If a task is too easy, it may become boring; if it is too difficult, it may cause anxiety. When the balance is right, individuals can become fully absorbed in what they are doing.

Engagement is important because it allows people to develop skills, experience satisfaction, and become more connected to their activities. It represents a form of well-being that is not based on emotion alone, but on deep involvement.

---

## Relationships

Relationships are a central part of well-being. Human beings depend on social connections for support, understanding, and a sense of belonging. Positive relationships can improve both emotional and psychological health.

Strong relationships are typically built on trust, communication, and mutual care. They provide individuals with emotional support during difficult times and contribute to positive experiences during everyday life.

In the PERMA model, relationships are considered essential because well-being is not only an individual experience. It is also shaped by interactions with others.

---

## Meaning

Meaning refers to the sense that one’s life has purpose and value. It involves feeling connected to something larger than oneself, such as a community, a belief system, or a set of personal values.

People often find meaning through contributing to others, pursuing important goals, or engaging in activities that reflect their values. Meaning can be especially important during challenging situations, as it helps individuals understand their experiences and maintain motivation.

Unlike positive emotion, meaning does not always involve feeling good. In some cases, meaningful activities can be difficult or stressful, but they still contribute to a deeper sense of well-being.

---

## Accomplishment

Accomplishment refers to the process of setting goals, working toward them, and achieving results. This element highlights the importance of progress, effort, and success.

People often pursue accomplishments for both internal and external reasons. Achieving goals can increase confidence, provide a sense of competence, and create a feeling of pride. At the same time, the process of working toward goals can be just as important as the outcome.

Accomplishment contributes to well-being by helping individuals feel capable and effective in their lives.

---

## The Interaction of the PERMA Elements

The five elements of PERMA are connected but not identical. A person may experience high levels of one element and lower levels of another. For example, someone may feel a strong sense of meaning but struggle with relationships, or they may achieve many goals but feel little engagement.

Because of this, well-being is best understood as a dynamic and changing process. The PERMA model allows individuals to reflect on different areas of their lives and identify where they may want to grow or make changes.

This multidimensional approach also suggests that improving well-being does not require focusing on only one area. Instead, individuals can strengthen different elements over time, depending on their needs and goals.

---

## Conclusion

The PERMA model provides a comprehensive framework for understanding well-being. By including positive emotion, engagement, relationships, meaning, and accomplishment, it moves beyond simple definitions of happiness and offers a more complex view of what it means to live well.

Rather than focusing only on feeling good, this model encourages individuals to build a balanced and meaningful life across multiple areas. Understanding PERMA can help individuals reflect on their experiences, make informed choices, and develop strategies to improve their well-being over time.
