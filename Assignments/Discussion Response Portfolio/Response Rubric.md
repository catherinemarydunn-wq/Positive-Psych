# Discussion Response Rubric (5% each)

Each discussion response is graded out of 5 marks.

---

## Criteria

| Level | Understanding of Concepts | Application & Examples | Critical Thinking | Clarity & Organization |
|------|--------------------------|-----------------------|------------------|------------------------|
| **5 (Excellent)** | Demonstrates clear, accurate understanding of key concepts; uses course ideas appropriately | Uses specific, relevant examples to support ideas | Shows thoughtful analysis, comparison, or evaluation; goes beyond basic ideas | Writing is clear, well-organized, and easy to follow |
| **4 (Good)** | Shows good understanding with minor errors or gaps | Provides relevant examples, though they may be somewhat general | Some analysis or reflection is present | Writing is mostly clear and organized |
| **3 (Satisfactory)** | Demonstrates basic understanding but may include some confusion or missing ideas | Examples are limited, unclear, or not fully developed | Mostly descriptive; limited analysis | Writing is somewhat unclear or unevenly organized |
| **2 (Limited)** | Shows minimal understanding; significant confusion or missing key ideas | Few or no relevant examples | Little to no analysis; ideas are very basic | Writing is unclear or difficult to follow |
| **1 (Very Limited)** | Does not demonstrate understanding of the topic | No relevant examples | No evidence of critical thinking | Writing is very unclear or incomplete |
| **0 (Incomplete)** | No submission or does not address the prompt | — | — | — |

---

## Scoring Guide

- 5 = Excellent  
- 4 = Good  
- 3 = Satisfactory  
- 2 = Limited  
- 1 = Very Limited  
- 0 = No submission  

---

## Notes for Instructors

- Focus on **ideas and understanding**, not grammar perfection  
- Accept minor language errors at the B2 level  
- Reward:
  - use of course concepts  
  - effort to explain ideas  
  - connection to examples  

---

## Notes for Students

To get a high grade:

- explain your ideas clearly  
- use examples  
- connect to course concepts  
- show your thinking (not just opinions)
