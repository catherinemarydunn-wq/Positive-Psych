# Research Critique (15%)

**Length:** 400–500 words *or equivalent*  
**Source:** One provided study  
**Due:** Week 6  

---

## Purpose

This assignment helps you:
- Understand how research in positive psychology is conducted  
- Evaluate the quality of a study  
- Think critically about research claims  

---

## Task (Flexible Options)

You will **analyze one provided study** and evaluate its strengths and limitations.

You can choose how you present your work:

### Option A – Written Response
- 400–500 words  

### Option B – Audio or Video Explanation
- 3–4 minutes  
- Include a short written reference  

### Option C – Visual + Written (Slides or Infographic)
- 5–7 slides *or* 1-page visual  
- Include short written explanations (approx. 250–300 words total)  

👉 All options must clearly evaluate the study using the categories below.

---

## What to Analyze

### 1. Research Question
- What is the study trying to find out?  
- Is the question clear and focused?  

---

### 2. Method
- How was the study conducted? (e.g., survey, experiment)  
- Who were the participants?  
- What data was collected?  

---

### 3. Correlation vs. Causation
- Does the study show correlation or causation?  
- Are the conclusions reasonable?  

---

### 4. Limitations
- What are the weaknesses of the study?  
- Are there problems with sample size, design, or measurement?  

---

### 5. Bias and/or Ethical Considerations
- Are there possible biases? (cultural, researcher, sampling)  
- Are there any ethical concerns?  

---

## Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

This assignment helps you:

- **Critically evaluate empirical research** in positive psychology  
- Distinguish **correlation vs. causation**  
- Identify **bias, limitations, and ethical issues**  
- Use **academic vocabulary** accurately  
- Communicate ideas clearly in academic formats  

---

## Support Options (UDL)

To support your learning, you can:

- Use the **research checklist from Week 3**  
- Review the study with a partner before writing  
- Use sentence starters or models provided in class  
- Submit a short outline for feedback (optional)  
- Use AI tools ethically to clarify ideas (Week 10)  

---

## Tips for Success

- Do not just describe the study — **evaluate it**  
- Use key terms: *correlation, causation, bias, limitation*  
- Be specific (avoid general statements like “the study is good”)  
- Keep your answer focused and organized

# Research Critique Rubric (15%)

## Overview
This rubric applies to all formats (written, audio/video, visual).  
Focus is on **critical thinking and clarity**, not format.

---

## Criteria and Levels

### 1. Understanding of Research (3 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 – Excellent** | Clear and accurate understanding of the study and its purpose |
| **2 – Developing** | General understanding; some missing or unclear details |
| **1 – Beginning** | Limited or inaccurate understanding of the study |

---

### 2. Analysis of Method (3 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 – Excellent** | Clearly explains method, participants, and data |
| **2 – Developing** | Basic explanation; some missing or unclear details |
| **1 – Beginning** | Limited or incorrect description of method |

---

### 3. Correlation vs. Causation (3 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 – Excellent** | Correctly identifies and clearly explains correlation vs. causation |
| **2 – Developing** | Partial understanding; some confusion |
| **1 – Beginning** | Incorrect or missing explanation |

---

### 4. Evaluation of Limitations and Bias (4 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 – Excellent** | Insightful evaluation of limitations, bias, and/or ethics |
| **3 – Good** | Clear evaluation with some detail |
| **2 – Developing** | Basic or general comments |
| **1 – Beginning** | Minimal or unclear evaluation |

---

### 5. Clarity and Use of Academic Language (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | Clear, organized, and appropriate academic vocabulary |
| **1 – Developing** | Some clarity; limited or inconsistent vocabulary |
| **0 – Beginning** | Unclear or difficult to follow |

---

## Total: /15

---

## What Success Looks Like

A strong critique:
- Clearly explains the study  
- Correctly identifies **correlation vs. causation**  
- Critically evaluates **limitations and bias**  
- Uses clear academic language
