# Intervention Project Rubric (25%)

## Overview
This rubric applies to both the **written report (15%)** and **presentation (10%)**.  
Focus is on **ideas, application, and clarity**, not format or design style.

---

# Part A: Report (15 marks)

## 1. Understanding and Use of Theory (4 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 – Excellent** | Clear, accurate explanation of theory; strong connection to intervention |
| **3 – Good** | Mostly clear; minor gaps in explanation or connection |
| **2 – Developing** | Basic understanding; weak or unclear connection to intervention |
| **1 – Beginning** | Limited or incorrect understanding of theory |

---

## 2. Intervention Design (4 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 – Excellent** | Clear, detailed, and realistic intervention; steps are easy to understand and practical |
| **3 – Good** | Clear idea; some detail but could be more specific or practical |
| **2 – Developing** | General or unclear design; lacks detail or feasibility |
| **1 – Beginning** | Intervention is unclear, unrealistic, or incomplete |

---

## 3. Use of Evidence (3 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **3 – Excellent** | 2–3 appropriate academic sources used effectively; correct APA |
| **2 – Developing** | Sources included but weakly integrated or with APA errors |
| **1 – Beginning** | Missing or inappropriate sources |

---

## 4. Cultural Considerations (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | Thoughtful discussion of cultural factors and inclusivity |
| **1 – Developing** | Basic or limited mention of culture |
| **0 – Beginning** | No meaningful consideration of culture |

---

## 5. Clarity and Organization (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | Clear, well-organized, easy to follow |
| **1 – Developing** | Some organization; ideas may be uneven or unclear |
| **0 – Beginning** | Difficult to follow; lacks structure |

---

## Report Total: /15

---

# Part B: Presentation (10 marks)

## 1. Content and Explanation (4 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **4 – Excellent** | Clearly explains problem, theory, and intervention |
| **3 – Good** | Mostly clear; some missing detail |
| **2 – Developing** | Basic explanation; lacks clarity or depth |
| **1 – Beginning** | Unclear or incomplete explanation |

---

## 2. Organization and Clarity (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | Well-organized and easy to follow |
| **1 – Developing** | Some organization but uneven |
| **0 – Beginning** | Difficult to follow |

---

## 3. Delivery (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | Clear speaking; confident; good pacing |
| **1 – Developing** | Some issues with clarity or pacing |
| **0 – Beginning** | Difficult to hear or understand |

---

## 4. Group Participation (2 marks)

| Level | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2 – Excellent** | All members contribute clearly |
| **1 – Developing** | Uneven participation |
| **0 – Beginning** | One or more members do not participate |

---

## Presentation Total: /10

---

# Overall Total: /25

---

## Notes for Different Formats (UDL)

- **Slides/Visuals:** Should support understanding (not too much text)  
- **Video:** Should be clear and organized  
- **Live Presentation:** Should stay within time (5–6 minutes)  

---

## What Success Looks Like

A strong project:
- Uses theory to explain a **real, specific problem**  
- Designs a **practical and realistic intervention**  
- Uses **research evidence**  
- Considers **cultural context**  
- Communicates ideas clearly as a group
