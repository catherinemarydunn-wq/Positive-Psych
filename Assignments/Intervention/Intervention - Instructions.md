# Intervention Project (25%)

**Format:** Group project (2–3 students)  
**Components:**  
- **Report:** 800–1000 words (15%)  
- **Presentation:** 5–6 minutes (10%)  
**Due:** Week 14  

---

## Purpose

This project helps you:
- Apply **positive psychology theories** to a real-life problem  
- Design a practical, **evidence-informed intervention**  
- Consider **cultural and social factors** in well-being  
- Communicate your ideas clearly in written and spoken formats  

---

## Task

In your group, you will design an **intervention** (a strategy or plan) to improve a **real student-life or early career challenge**.

Your intervention must:
- Be based on **positive psychology theory**  
- Use **research evidence**  
- Include **cultural considerations**  

---

## Step 1: Choose a Problem

Select a **specific, realistic challenge**, such as:

- Stress during exams  
- Lack of motivation  
- Burnout  
- Social isolation  
- Group work conflict  
- Language barriers in academic settings  

👉 Be specific (not just “stress” → “stress during midterms while working part-time”)

---

## Step 2: Theoretical Grounding

Choose **1–2 theories** from the course, such as:

- PERMA  
- Broaden-and-Build Theory  
- Self-Determination Theory  
- Self-compassion  
- Character strengths  

Explain:
- What the theory is  
- How it helps explain the problem  
- Why it supports your intervention  

---

## Step 3: Design the Intervention

Describe your intervention clearly:

- What is it? (e.g., workshop, habit, program, strategy)  
- Who is it for?  
- What are the steps?  
- How often does it happen?  

👉 Your intervention should be **realistic and practical**

---

## Step 4: Use Evidence

- Include **2–3 academic sources** (course readings encouraged)  
- Explain how research supports your intervention  
- Use **APA citation**  

---

## Step 5: Cultural Considerations

Explain:
- How culture may influence the problem or solution  
- How your intervention is inclusive or adaptable  
- Any limitations (e.g., not all strategies work for all cultures)  

---

## Step 6: Reflection (Short Section)

Briefly explain:
- Why your intervention is useful  
- Any challenges or limitations  

---

## Report Structure (Recommended)

**1. Introduction**
- Describe the problem  

**2. Theoretical Framework**
- Explain the theory  

**3. Intervention Design**
- Describe your solution  

**4. Evidence**
- Support with research  

**5. Cultural Considerations**
- Discuss inclusivity and context  

**6. Conclusion**
- Summarize your idea  

---

## Presentation (5–6 minutes)

Your group will present your intervention to the class.

### You can choose your format:
- Slides (recommended)  
- Poster or visual  
- Short video  

### Your presentation should include:
- The problem  
- The theory  
- Your intervention  
- Cultural considerations  

👉 Each group member should speak.

## Tips for Success

- Keep your intervention **simple and realistic**  
- Clearly connect your ideas to **theory**  
- Use **specific examples**  
- Consider **different cultural perspectives**  
- Practice your presentation to stay within time  

---

## What Is an Intervention?

An intervention is:
> A planned action designed to improve well-being or solve a problem.

Examples:
- A weekly gratitude journaling routine  
- A peer support group for stress  
- A goal-setting system for students  
- A self-compassion practice for burnout
